Llamas and Aplacas

Llamas

  • Llamas are able to learn to understand and perform basic tasks.
  • A llama's lifespan is 20 years.
  • Llamas are very social and live in herds.
  • LLamas can grow to be 6 feet tall!
  • Humming is one of the ways llamas communicate.
  • LLamas are used as therapy animals to work in hospitals and schools.
  • National Llama Day is December 9th.
  • Llamas can shoot green spit up to 10 feet!
  • Llamas are vegetarians with highly efficient digestive systems.
  • Llamas are close relatives of alpacas.

Alpacas

  • Alpacas resemble a small llama in appearance.
  • Alpacas are kept in herds that graze on the level altitudes.
  • Unlike llamas, alpacas were not bred to be beasts of burden, but were bred specifically for their fiber.
  • Alpaca fiber is used for making knitted and woven items, similar to wool.
  • Alpacas have been domesticated for thousands of years.
  • Alpacas are social herd animals that live in family groups.
  • Not all alpacas spit, but all are capable of doing so.
  • Most alpacas do not like being grabbed.
  • When they are in danger, alpacas make a high-pitched, shrieking whine.
  • Along with camels and llamas, alpacas are classified as camelids.
